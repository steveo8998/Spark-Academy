Section 2: What Your Tour Looks Like

A tour at Spark takes about 30 minutes. There's no script and no sales pitch — just a personal walk through the classrooms with Dr. Michelle Peterson, Ed.D., who founded and leads the school.

Here's what you'll see and do:

Walk through every classroom. You'll see the learning spaces set up for the age group your child would join — including the Developmental Playroom, which transforms with a new theme every month.

Watch purposeful play in action. If your visit falls during class hours, you'll observe real learning happening — children building, creating, collaborating, and problem-solving with teacher guidance.

Ask anything. Whether you're curious about the daily schedule, the curriculum, how teachers handle big emotions, or how Spark supports children with different learning styles — bring your questions. That's what this visit is for.

Leave with clarity. You'll walk out knowing whether Spark is the right fit for your child and your family. No pressure, no follow-up calls unless you want them.

Strategic Note "How Spark supports children with different learning styles" gives Megan (Persona 3) explicit permission to ask about individual learning differences — per the Parent Personas doc. "No follow-up calls unless you want them" addresses a common concern about giving a phone number to a school.

Section 6: Questions About Visiting Spark

Strategic Note Dual purpose: (1) removes last-minute hesitations that prevent form submission, (2) generates FAQPage schema for AEO — making this page citable by AI search tools.
Q: How long does a tour take?
Most tours last about 30 minutes. If you have more questions, we're happy to take as much time as you need.
Q: Should I bring my child?
You're welcome to bring your child, but it's not required. Some parents prefer to visit first on their own so they can focus on asking questions — and that's completely fine. If your child does come along, they're welcome to explore the classroom.
Q: What will I see during the tour?
You'll walk through all of our learning spaces, including the classrooms for your child's age group and the Developmental Playroom. If you visit during class hours, you'll see learning in action. Dr. Peterson leads every tour personally, so you'll have a chance to ask questions directly.
Q: Can both parents come?
Absolutely. We encourage it. Choosing a preschool is a big decision, and we want both parents (or any caregivers involved in the decision) to feel confident and informed.
Q: What if I'm not ready to enroll after the tour?
No pressure at all. Some families tour months before they're ready to enroll. Some visit more than once. We're here whenever you're ready — and if questions come up later, you can always reach out.

Section 7: Not Ready to Tour? That's Okay.

If you have a question but aren't quite ready to schedule a visit, we'd love to hear from you. Send us your question and someone from our team will respond personally.

Design Decision Included with clear visual separation below the FAQ. A parent who scrolls past the tour form and reads the FAQ but still isn't ready needs a lower-commitment exit ramp.
