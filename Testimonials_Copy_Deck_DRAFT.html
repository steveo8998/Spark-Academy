Page Design Principles

The testimonials ARE the content. This is the one page where Spark's voice steps aside and lets parents speak. The copy scaffolding — hero intro, section headers, CTA — exists only to frame the testimonials and guide the visitor toward action.

Design for two today, ten tomorrow. The page launches with Holly and April's testimonials. The structure must feel complete with two and feel rich with ten. Use a clean, stacked layout where each testimonial is a self-contained block. Adding a third or tenth means adding another block — no redesign required.

Every testimonial earns its page placement. As testimonials are collected, each should be evaluated for which page it serves best. This page is the comprehensive collection. Program pages feature the single best-fit testimonial with a "Read more from Spark families" link driving here.

Section 1: Hero (Above the Fold)

H1 (Page Title)

Real Families. Real Experiences.
Strategic Note: The H1 avoids "What Parents Are Saying" (generic, school-centric) and "Testimonials" (clinical, expected). "Real Families. Real Experiences." is child-centric because it centers the family's lived experience — not the school's collection of praise.

Hero Subheadline

The families who walk through our doors every morning are the most honest measure of who we are. Here's what Spark Academy looks like through their eyes.

Strategic Note: "Through their eyes" reinforces the 90/10 child-centric lens. The school isn't telling you it's great — the families are showing you.

Section 2: Parent Testimonials

Design Direction: Each testimonial appears as a standalone block with generous white space. Include the parent's first name and "Spark Academy Parent." No star ratings — these are narrative testimonials, not review snippets. Each block should feel like a personal letter, not a product review.
Ordering Principle: Lead with the testimonial that best represents the broadest Spark experience. As the collection grows, organize by the parent concern each testimonial addresses — readiness, academics, social-emotional growth, inclusivity, belonging.

Testimonial 1 — Holly

"One thing my husband and I strive to teach and instill in him is curiosity, and Spark is the perfect place for this, along with an excellent academic curriculum! Since beginning at Spark, our son has begun to notice and identify letters while we are out and about and has become more interested in them and their meaning. He has also made friends there and is learning awesome social and emotional skills that we are encouraging at home. Spark is a wonderful partner with us as we seek together to spur on our son's love of learning and exploration."
— Holly, Spark Academy Parent
Strategic Note: Holly leads the page for three reasons: (1) She describes a specific, observable academic outcome (letter recognition) — this is the proof David & Priya evaluate for. (2) She names social and emotional growth alongside academics — the "both/and" message. (3) She positions Spark as a "partner," which mirrors the child-centric framing of the entire site.

Testimonial 2 — April

"Enrolling my son at Spark Academy has been the best decision I've made. Michelle and her entire team truly meet each child at their level and begin to foster a love of learning. I appreciate their use of developmental play and enrichment activities such as art and music/movement to augment traditional classroom education. The experience and expertise they offer are top-notch! But most importantly, my son absolutely loves his school and all of his teachers. I look forward to continuing to watch him learn and grow with the help of Spark Academy!"
— April, Spark Academy Parent
Strategic Note: April's testimonial addresses a different set of parent concerns than Holly's: (1) "Meet each child at their level" is the inclusivity signal that resonates with Megan (Persona 3). (2) She specifically names developmental play and enrichment activities, validating the Purposeful Play Framework. (3) "My son absolutely loves his school" is the emotional close.

Section 3: Testimonial Collection Priorities

As Dr. Peterson collects new parent testimonials, the following types should be prioritized:

  1. 1. A Kindergarten or K-Prep parent — addresses the "is my child ready?" anxiety and validates the academic rigor for the oldest programs.
  2. 2. A parent who transferred from another school — "we switched to Spark because..." is the most powerful competitive proof point available.
  3. 3. A Camp Play or Discovery Station parent — extends social proof beyond core preschool programs.
  4. 4. A parent who started with Fresh 3's Fridays — validates the entry-point pathway and reassures hesitant parents that Friday is "real" Spark.
  5. 5. A second- or third-year family — longevity testimonials signal retention and satisfaction in a way single-year reviews cannot.

Dr. Peterson can request testimonials informally during parent conferences, at the end of the school year, or after Camp Play concludes. A simple email template is all that's needed. Written permission should be captured via email reply.
