Section 01

Hero

H1 (Page Title)
Insights from a Researcher, Educator, and Founder
Strategic Note
Hybrid lens. The H1 establishes Dr. Peterson's triple identity — researcher (school-centric credential), educator (warm and relatable), and founder (authority anchor) — without naming the school or sounding institutional. It works for both audiences: parents hear "someone who knows what she's talking about," and educators hear "a peer with credentials." The word "Insights" signals ongoing value (a blog worth returning to), not a static information page. Primary keyword is not forced into the H1 because this page does not target a competitive keyword — clarity and brand positioning take priority here.

Hero Subheadline

Dr. Michelle Peterson, Ed.D., writes about what the research says, what the classroom shows, and what it means for your child.

Strategic Note
Child-centric close ("what it means for your child") anchors the subheadline in the parent's world while the credential (Ed.D.) and the research/classroom pairing establish E-E-A-T in a single sentence. This subheadline doubles as the blog's elevator pitch — if a parent reads nothing else on this page, they understand who writes the content, why it's credible, and why it matters to them.
Section 02

Category Filter

The blog hub displays two primary category filters as tabs or pill-style buttons directly below the hero. These are the primary navigational control for the page.

Filter Labels

Filter Label Behavior
Default For Parents Shows parent-facing posts. Tour CTA visible. Consulting CTA hidden.
Secondary For Educators Shows educator-facing posts. "Work with Dr. Peterson" CTA visible. Tour CTA hidden.
All All Articles Shows both audiences. Tour CTA visible (parent-default). Consulting CTA hidden.
Strategic Note
No filter is labeled "All" by default — the page loads with "For Parents" active because parents are the majority audience (per dual-funnel architecture). An "All Articles" option exists for users who want both, but it is not the default state. This prevents parents from ever seeing educator-facing content first, which could create confusion about what Spark Academy is. The "For Educators" filter is the consulting funnel's blog entry point per the Consulting Funnel Wireframe Section 8.

Filter Micro-Copy (Conditional)

When "For Parents" is active:

"Expert guidance on preschool readiness, child development, and choosing the right program for your family."

When "For Educators" is active:

"Research-grounded perspectives on curriculum design, inclusive classrooms, and early childhood program leadership."

When "All Articles" is active:

No micro-copy displayed — the H1 and subheadline provide sufficient context.

Strategic Note
The micro-copy serves two purposes: it confirms the visitor is in the right place after clicking a filter, and it front-loads audience-specific keywords for crawlers. The parent micro-copy uses child-centric language ("your family"). The educator micro-copy uses professional vocabulary ("curriculum design," "inclusive classrooms") that signals this content is for peers, not parents — reinforcing the Vocabulary Firewall from the Brand Voice Guide.
Section 03

Featured Post Area

A single featured post appears above the main post grid, visually distinct (larger card, hero image, excerpt). This is a pinned position controlled manually in the CMS — not algorithmic.

Featured Post Logic

One featured post at a time. Swapped manually when a new cornerstone article publishes or when a seasonal priority shifts (e.g., Camp Play registration opens).

The featured post respects the active filter. If "For Educators" is active and the pinned post is parent-facing, the featured area either shows the top educator post instead or collapses. Recommended behavior: show the most recent post matching the active filter in the featured position when the pinned post doesn't match.

Launch featured post: "Daycare vs. Preschool: What's the Real Difference?" (Article 2 from Blog Content Roadmap — cornerstone article, 9,000/mo addressable volume, highest-impact first publish alongside the tax deductibility post).

Featured Post Card Elements

Element Specification
Featured image Full-width or 60/40 split with excerpt. Larger than standard post cards.
Post title (H2) Linked to full post. Playfair Display heading.
Excerpt 2–3 sentences. Hand-written (not auto-generated) for cornerstone articles.
Author byline Dr. Michelle Peterson, Ed.D. (see Post Card Spec for full format)
Category tag "For Parents" or "For Educators" — visible on card.
Read button "Read the Full Article →" — linked to post URL.
Section 04

Post Grid & Post Card Spec

Below the featured post, all remaining posts display in a responsive grid (3-column desktop, 2-column tablet, 1-column mobile). Posts are ordered by publish date, most recent first. Pagination or infinite scroll — Phase 4 design decision; copy deck is agnostic.

Post Card Spec

Every post in the grid uses a consistent card format. This spec defines the content architecture for each card — the visual design is a Phase 4 deliverable.

Card Element Content Notes
Featured image Post's featured image 16:9 aspect ratio recommended. WebP format. Lazy-loaded.
Category tag "For Parents" or "For Educators" Small pill/badge. Visible on every card. Color-coded (Phase 4 decision).
Post title Full post title (H3 on hub page) Linked to full post. Truncated at 2 lines if needed.
Excerpt Auto-generated excerpt, 120–160 characters WordPress auto-excerpt or custom excerpt field. Ends with "…" if truncated.
Author byline By Dr. Michelle Peterson, Ed.D. Consistent on all cards. Links to /dr-michelle-peterson/.
Publish date Month Day, Year format Displays below byline. Keeps content feeling current.
Read more link "Read More →" Text link, not a button. Entire card is also clickable (whole-card link pattern).

Educator Post Card Variation

Posts tagged "For Educators" use a slightly modified byline to signal consulting authority:

Educator byline: "By Dr. Michelle Peterson, Ed.D. — Educator, Researcher, Consultant"

Strategic Note
Per the Consulting Funnel Wireframe Section 8, the educator byline adds "Consultant" to signal that Dr. Peterson is available for professional engagement — not just writing about education. The byline still links to /dr-michelle-peterson/, which contains the "For Educators" consulting bridge section. This is subtle but intentional: the card itself is a micro-funnel touchpoint.

Branded Series Display

Below the main post grid, the blog hub surfaces the three branded series from the Blog Content Roadmap as distinct visual groupings. Each series appears as a horizontal card row or carousel with its own heading, brief description, and 3–4 post cards.

Series Specifications

Strategic Note
Series sections only appear once enough posts exist to populate them (minimum 2 posts per series before the section renders). At launch, these sections will likely be hidden — the Blog Content Roadmap schedules series posts starting in Phase 2 (months 7–12). The series descriptions use child-centric language for parent series and hybrid language for Ask Dr. Peterson, mirroring the lens assignments from the Blog Content Roadmap. The Homeschool Hub series directly supports Discovery Station enrollment.

Series "View All" Link

Each series section includes a "View All [Series Name] Articles →" link that filters the post grid to show only that series. Implementation: WordPress tag or custom taxonomy. Phase 5 decision.

Section 08

Schema Markup & Tracking

Schema Specification

The blog hub uses CollectionPage schema (auto-generated by RankMath for archive pages) plus sitewide BreadcrumbList. No FAQPage schema on this page — there is no FAQ content here. Individual blog posts carry their own Article + FAQPage schema per the AEO blog post template.

Breadcrumb

Home > Blog

GA4 / GTM Tracking

Category filter clicks tracked as custom events: blog_filter_click with parameter filter_value ("parents", "educators", "all").

Post card clicks tracked: blog_post_click with parameter post_title and post_category.

CTA button clicks tracked per sitewide CTA tracking spec: data-cta-type="tour" or data-cta-type="consulting" and data-cta-text matching the button label.

Series section visibility and click-through tracked: blog_series_click with parameter series_name.

Strategic Note
The filter click tracking is especially valuable for understanding audience composition. If 30% of blog hub visitors click "For Educators," the consulting content strategy is generating meaningful traffic and the educator publishing cadence may warrant acceleration. This data feeds directly into the monthly KPI review per the Blog Content Roadmap.
