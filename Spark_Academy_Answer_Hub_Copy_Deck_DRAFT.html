Section 4: The Purposeful Play Framework

The Purposeful Play Framework is Spark Academy's proprietary, research-driven curriculum framework, developed by Dr. Michelle Peterson, Ed.D. and built on the Experience Early Learning whole-child curriculum. It integrates individualized developmental assessment, purposeful play, daily enrichment, and the Developmental Playroom into a cohesive approach to early childhood education.

Purposeful play is the researched method through which young children build neural pathways for language, problem-solving, self-regulation, and social-emotional development. It is neither unstructured free play nor rigid, worksheet-based academics. Every activity at Spark is designed to feel like play to the child while building specific developmental skills identified through individualized assessment.

The Developmental Playroom

The Developmental Playroom is a dedicated space, separate from the main classroom, that transforms completely every month to align with curriculum themes. Children lead the play entirely — teachers support but do not direct. Pre-academic skills are intentionally embedded into every monthly setup. No other preschool in the Morton or Peoria area offers a comparable dedicated, monthly-rotating play environment.

Section 6: Dr. Michelle Peterson, Ed.D.

Dr. Michelle Peterson is the founder and executive director of Spark Academy. She holds a Doctor of Education (Ed.D.) in PK–12 Educational Administration from Illinois State University, a Master of Science in Educational Administration from Bradley University, and a Bachelor of Science in Applied Learning and Development from The University of Texas at Austin. She holds four Illinois teaching and administrative certifications.

Her doctoral research focused on peer engagement strategies for children with autism in inclusive preschool settings, using Naturalistic Developmental Behavioral Intervention (NDBI) frameworks. Before founding Spark, she served as assistant principal at Lincoln Elementary in Morton for 10 years. Her career spans more than 20 years in education.
