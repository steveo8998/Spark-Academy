Strategic Note
This page serves the broadest audience of any Spark program page. The parent reading it has a school-age child — possibly in public school, private school, or homeschool — and is looking for something more for their kid after the final bell. The emotional driver isn't academic readiness (that's the preschool pages). It's access: their child wants to try things — theater, cooking, sewing, art — that their school doesn't offer, or doesn't offer well. The copy leads with what the child gets to do, not what Spark is. The page must also be built as a flexible content container: the featured program changes each semester, and the page structure must accommodate new listings without a full redesign. This is a shorter, tighter page than Discovery Station because it's a simpler program — one class, one hour, one day per week.

Section 02

What After-School Enrichment Is

Section Headline (H2)
One Hour a Week That Could Become Their Favorite Part of the Week

After-School Enrichment at Spark Academy is a semester-based program where kids get to dive into something they love — or discover something they didn't know they'd love. Each semester features a rotating program: it might be a theater production, a cooking series, a game club, a Lego building challenge, or a music and art experience. The program changes, but the format stays the same: one hour, every Tuesday, for twelve weeks.

This isn't homework help. It's not tutoring. It's the kind of hands-on, creative, collaborative learning that gives children the chance to build skills their regular school day may not touch — confidence on stage, problem-solving in the kitchen, teamwork around a game board, or the pride of finishing a project they built from scratch.

Open to everyone. Public school, private school, homeschool — any child in the age range is welcome. Some of our Discovery Station homeschool students stay for After-School Enrichment on the same Tuesday, making it a seamless afternoon of enrichment and connection.

Discovery Station Homeschool Enrichment → /homeschool-enrichment/
Strategic Note
The "not homework help" distinction is critical. Parents searching for after-school programs often encounter tutoring centers (Kumon, Mathnasium, Sylvan). Spark's program is an entirely different category — experiential enrichment, not academic remediation. Naming what it isn't positions what it is more clearly.

Section 07

Frequently Asked Questions

Section Headline (H2)
Questions About After-School Enrichment
What ages can participate?
After-School Enrichment is designed for school-age children, generally ages 5–11 and up. The specific age range may vary slightly depending on the semester's program. Call us for details on the current offering.
Is this only for Spark Academy students?
No. After-School Enrichment is open to all children — public school, private school, and homeschool. Your child does not need to be enrolled in any other Spark program to participate.
What time does the program run?
Every Tuesday from 4:00 to 5:00 PM, for twelve weeks per semester.
Do programs change each semester?
Yes. Each semester features a different program — it could be theater, cooking, art, games, or something else entirely. Call 309-291-3292 to find out what's running right now and what's coming next.
Is there a performance or showcase at the end?
It depends on the program. Theater semesters include a live performance. Other programs may feature a showcase, exhibit, or culminating project. We'll let you know what to expect when you register.
Can my homeschool child attend Discovery Station and After-School Enrichment on the same day?
Yes. Discovery Station classes run on Tuesday and Thursday afternoons, and After-School Enrichment starts at 4:00 PM on Tuesdays. Some families do exactly this — their child attends Discovery Station classes and stays for the after-school program. It makes Tuesday a full afternoon of enrichment and connection.
