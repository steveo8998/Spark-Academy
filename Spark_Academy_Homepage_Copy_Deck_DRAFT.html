Strategic Note
The hero must accomplish three things within the first viewport: (1) child-centric emotional hook that mirrors the parent's aspiration, (2) Dr. Peterson's Ed.D. credential visible as the positioning statement, and (3) a clear, inviting primary CTA. Parents decide within 10 seconds whether to stay. The headline bank provides "Where curious minds become confident learners" — it activates the aspiration trigger (Kindergarten Confidence pillar) while keeping the child as the protagonist.

Hero Body Copy

Your child's first school experience shapes everything that follows — how they see themselves as learners, how they build friendships, how they walk into kindergarten. At Spark Academy, every part of their day is designed with that in mind.

Founded by Dr. Michelle Peterson, Ed.D., Spark Academy brings doctoral-level expertise to a warm, homelike setting where your child is known by name and nurtured by design.

Strategic Note
Hybrid lens. Opens child-centric (the child's trajectory), transitions to school-centric (Dr. Peterson's credential). The phrase "known by name and nurtured by design" echoes the Known & Nurtured pillar and maps directly to Megan's (Persona 3) need for individualized attention.

Section 04

The Purposeful Play Framework

Section Headline (H2)

Every Moment Is Designed with Purpose

At Spark, play isn't recess. It's the research-proven method through which young children build the neural pathways for language, problem-solving, and self-regulation.

The Purposeful Play Framework — developed by Dr. Michelle Peterson and built on the Experience Early Learning curriculum — structures every part of your child's day around intentional, play-based learning experiences. From the Developmental Play Room that transforms monthly to daily enrichment in Spanish, STEM, art, communication, and music — nothing at Spark is accidental.

Strategic Note
The "neural pathways" argument comes directly from Dr. Peterson's video call. It transforms play from "soft" to "neuroscience." Naming the framework signals methodology, not just philosophy. Listing five specific enrichment subjects makes value tangible — every competitor says "enrichment"; none list five subjects delivered five days a week.

The space should look designed, not random. Vertical or square. Natural light. Alt: "Child exploring the Developmental Play Room at Spark Academy, a monthly rotating play-based learning environment"

Meet Dr. Michelle Peterson

Section Headline (H2)

Led by a Researcher. Built by a Mother.

Dr. Michelle Peterson didn't just study early childhood education — she built a school around what the research says works.

With a doctorate in education from Illinois State University, 20 years in the classroom and school administration, and four Illinois teaching certifications, Dr. Peterson founded Spark Academy to close the gap between what research proves about how young children learn and what most preschools actually do.

When you visit Spark, you won't find worksheets from 1987. You'll find a learning environment that's been reimagined from the ground up — where every enrichment, every classroom setup, and every interaction is grounded in evidence and delivered with care.

Strategic Note
The "worksheets from 1987" reference comes directly from Dr. Peterson's origin story. It's specific, visual, slightly humorous, and immediately communicates why Spark exists. The credential stack (doctorate + 20 years + 4 certifications) follows the funnel wireframe's trust signal placement.
