Strategic Note
This is a wayfinding page, not a conversion page. Its primary job is to reduce confusion, help parents identify the right program for their child's age, and route them to the appropriate individual page where conversion happens. Copy is intentionally tighter and less narrative than the individual program pages — the goal is clarity and orientation, not persuasion. The developmental continuity arc (a child can grow with Spark from age 3 through kindergarten) is the emotional thread that holds the overview together.
Section 01

Hero (Above the Fold)

H1 — Page Title

A Program for Every Age. One School for Every Beginning.

Strategic Note
Hybrid lens — "every age" is child-centric (there's a place for your child, wherever they are), and "one school" is school-centric (Spark is the consistent thread). The word "beginning" connects to the brand tagline ("Purposeful Play. Exceptional Beginnings.") without repeating it verbatim. This H1 is designed for families with multiple children — a household with a 3-year-old and a 5-year-old can see immediately that Spark serves both.

Hero Subheadline

From your child's very first Friday to the last day of kindergarten — Spark Academy offers programs designed for every stage of early childhood. All rooted in the same doctoral-led, play-based approach. All in one community.

Hero CTA Block

Not sure which program fits your child? Use the age guide below — or give us a call at 309-291-3292.

📸 Photo Direction — Hero
A wide shot of children from multiple age groups — a 3-year-old, a couple of 4-year-olds, and a 5-year-old — each engaged in different activities that represent different program stages. The image communicates range and continuity. Warm, natural light. Homelike environment. This is the one page where showing multiple ages together is the right call.

What Every Program Has in Common

H2: The Same Foundation. Every Day. Across Every Program.

Regardless of which program your child is in, every day at Spark Academy includes the same core structure:

The Purposeful Play Framework Dr. Peterson's doctoral-research-informed curriculum approach, built on the Experience Early Learning whole-child model. Every activity in every program is designed with developmental intent.
The Developmental Playroom A dedicated, monthly-rotating play environment unlike anything else in the area. Student-driven, theme-aligned, and changed out completely every month. Exclusive to Spark.
Five Daily Enrichments Spanish on Monday, STEM on Tuesday, Art on Wednesday, Communication on Thursday, Music & Movement on Friday. Not extras — core programming, built into every schedule.
A 5:1 Student-to-Teacher Ratio Maximum 10–12 students per class. The lowest ratio of any preschool program in the area.
Credentialed Teachers Every Spark teacher holds a degree and credential in education.
All-Inclusive Tuition No supply lists. No hidden fees. No additional costs. Everything is included.
Strategic Note
This section is the one place on the overview page where school-centric proof is appropriate at a higher concentration. It's the "why Spark across all programs" layer — the consistent differentiators that no competitor can claim together. The all-inclusive callout belongs here because parents comparing programs across multiple schools are particularly attuned to total cost.
Section 04

A Child Can Grow With Spark

H2: From Their Third Birthday Through Kindergarten — One Community

One of the most meaningful things about Spark Academy's program structure is continuity.

A child can begin with Fresh 3's Fridays the day they turn three. They can grow into the full 3's program. They can deepen their experience through the 4's and Kindergarten Prep years. And they can complete their early childhood education in Spark's private Kindergarten — surrounded by teachers they know, in a community that has been part of their family's life for years.

Not every child follows every step. Many families enter at 4 or at Kindergarten Prep. All are warmly welcomed wherever they begin. But for families who find their way to Spark early, the continuity of relationships, expectations, and environment across multiple years is something no single-year program can replicate.
