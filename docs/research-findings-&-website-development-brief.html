SECTION 2

The Six Rules That Will Govern the New Website

These aren't opinions or preferences. Every one of these rules appeared as a gap in at least 8 of the 11 schools we studied. They are the patterns that separate sites that generate leads from sites that just exist.

1Make it easy to visit before you ask them to apply. Every single school we studied pushed parents straight to an application — high commitment, high friction. Not one offered a simple, above-the-fold 'Schedule a Tour' as the primary CTA. That white space belongs to Spark. A 3-field tour booking form above the fold is the single biggest conversion advantage available in this market.
2Authority only converts when it's visible. The International Preschools has a Tom Brokaw-narrated video, 62 years of history, and three-generation families — and buries all of it on sub-pages. Dr. Peterson's Ed.D., founding story, and research background must appear on the homepage, near every CTA, and on every page where a parent is deciding. Credentials in a drawer convert nobody.
3One precise sentence beats a page of philosophy. REDS positions their entire school in six words: 'NYC's only AMI-recognized school.' It answers who they are, why they're different, and why it matters simultaneously. Spark needs its equivalent — a single sentence only this school could say. Not 'play-based preschool in Morton.' Something no one else can claim.
4Testimonials are the conversion mechanism, not the decoration. Playgarden's homepage leads with nine detailed parent narratives organized by concern type — academic preparation, social growth, teacher warmth, kindergarten transition. These testimonials do more conversion work than every credential and accreditation on the site combined. Parents don't trust institutions. They trust other parents.
5The blog is the engine that powers everything else. Of 11 schools studied, not one is publishing keyword-targeted, authority-building blog content authored by a credentialed researcher. In the entire greater Peoria market, no competitor is doing this at all. When Dr. Peterson publishes 2–4 articles per month on topics she has literally researched at the doctoral level, Spark builds a search position that no local competitor can challenge — or buy.
6Local pages win local searches. Both Playgarden (NYC) and Bubbles Academy (Chicago) use location-specific landing pages targeting neighborhood-level searches. For Spark, this means dedicated pages for Morton, Washington, Peoria, East Peoria, Germantown Hills, and Metamora — each one targeting the search queries families in that community are actually typing. 'Best preschool Morton IL' is a high-value search with essentially zero competition right now.

Why Two Separate Funnels? The parent funnel and the consulting funnel share the same website and the same blog — but they have completely separate conversion paths, separate forms, and separate tracking. This matters for two reasons. First, a parent visiting the site should never stumble into consulting content and wonder if they're in the right place. The navigation keeps them in their lane. Second, Stephen needs to know whether a new inquiry came from a parent searching for preschool or an educator searching for consulting — because those two people need completely different follow-up. Separate funnels make that measurement possible. And here's the bonus: a consulting page does double authority duty. Every parent who sees that other educators hire Dr. Peterson for her expertise becomes more confident in their own enrollment decision.

SECTION 5

How a Visitor Becomes a Family

This is the journey we're engineering for every parent who finds the Spark Academy website. Every page, every CTA, and every piece of content plays a specific role in moving a family from 'just browsing' to 'ready to visit.'

1Discovery (Google Search)A Morton or Peoria parent searches 'preschool near me,' 'play-based preschool Peoria,' or 'is my 3-year-old ready for school.' A blog post or local landing page is the first result they find.Organic search
2First Impression (Homepage)The homepage makes a strong above-the-fold impression in under 3 seconds. Dr. Peterson's name and credential are visible. The primary CTA — 'Schedule a Tour' — is front and center.3-second impression
3Consideration (Program + About Pages)The parent explores. They read Dr. Peterson's story. They read the program page for their child's age. They read two or three parent testimonials. Each page answers a concern and deepens confidence.Building trust
4Conversion (Tour Booking)The parent clicks 'Schedule a Tour.' A dedicated page with a 3-field form (name, child's age, preferred date). Low friction. Dr. Peterson's welcome message. One click to submit.Form submission
5Confirmation (Thank-You Page)A personalized thank-you page confirms the request, sets expectations ('we'll call within 24 hours'), and offers a next step ('while you wait, read this'). Provide common questions a parent needs to ask to discover if their kid is ready for preschool.Nurture begins
6Enrollment (The Visit)The family visits. They meet Dr. Peterson. They see the classroom. The website did its job — now the school takes over.Spark converts

SECTION 6

What Happens Next

The research is done. The strategy is defined. Here's what we build from here — in order.

1Michelle Completes the Content Brief Stephen has prepared a separate document with guided writing assignments for every major page. Michelle works through those prompts — in any format, at any pace — and her answers become the foundation for all copy. Nothing gets written without her voice in it.
2Brand Voice & Messaging Guide Before any page copy is written, Stephen and Claude develop a Brand Voice Guide — the document that governs how Spark Academy sounds across every page, every blog post, and every CTA. Dr. Peterson reviews and approves it before anything else is written.
3Keyword Research & SEO Map A full keyword research report assigns specific search terms to every page in the sitemap above, along with search volume and competition data for the Peoria market. This confirms the URL structure and content priorities before build begins.
4Content Creation Page copy is written for every section of the site — starting with Homepage, About Dr. Peterson, and the Tour page. Each draft is sent to Michelle for review with clear explanation of the strategy behind every choice.
5Design & Build With content in place, the site is designed and built on a platform that Stephen can update seasonally — new programs, summer camp schedules, blog posts — using Claude to assist. Analytics, call tracking, and form tracking are configured from day one so every lead can be measured.
The goal is simple. When a family in Morton, Washington, Peoria, or East Peoria searches for a preschool — the first result they find should be Spark Academy. When they arrive, the site should make them feel like they've already found the right place. And when they submit a tour request, it should feel effortless. That's what we're building.
