Strategic Context

Four is the year when kindergarten readiness starts to feel real to parents. The 3's page answers "Is my child ready?" The 4's page answers "Is my child getting what they need to succeed?" Parents here are more academically aware, more schedule-conscious, and more likely to be comparing Spark against other pre-K options. That question, answered clearly, is the conversion engine for this page.

Section 2 · What Changes at Four
Four Is the Bridge Year — and How You Cross It Matters

Something shifts at four. Children who spent their 3's year learning how to be in school — how to sit, listen, share, and take turns — arrive at four ready to do more with that foundation. Their vocabulary is exploding. They're asking "why" about everything. They're forming real friendships and navigating the complexity of them.

This is the pre-kindergarten year. And at Spark, we take that seriously.

The 4's program builds directly on the Purposeful Play Framework, deepening the academic play, enrichment, and developmental playroom experiences your child already knows. But four-year-olds are developmentally ready for more — more sustained engagement, more complex challenges, more independence — and the 4's program is calibrated for exactly that.

Dr. Peterson's doctoral research in early childhood education isn't a credential we list to look impressive. It's the reason the 4's program is structured the way it is. She knows what four-year-old brains need and what they can handle — and that knowledge is built into every part of the day.

Section 4 · How Many Days Is Right for Your Child?
Two Days, Three Days, or Five — Here's How to Think About It

The 4's program offers three scheduling options, and choosing between them is one of the most common questions families bring to their tour. Here's an honest, developmentally grounded way to think through it.

Two Days a Week
T/Th — $350/month

Two days is a strong start and a complete program. Your child receives the full Purposeful Play curriculum, the daily enrichment rotation, and the Developmental Playroom experience — just on two days a week. This is a great option for families with younger siblings at home, families managing complex schedules, or children who are still building stamina for structured learning environments.

Three Days a Week
M/W/F — $450/month

Three days is where many families land — the sweet spot between scheduling flexibility and developmental depth. An additional day means more time with peers, more repetitions of the enrichment cycle, and more sustained engagement with curriculum themes. Children who attend three days a week often show noticeably stronger peer relationships by mid-year simply because they have more time to build them.

Five Days a Week
M–F — $600/month

Five days is the fullest experience Spark offers for four-year-olds. It's ideal for children who are energized by school, have demonstrated strong readiness, and whose families are preparing for the routine of daily kindergarten the following year. Five days at Spark is the most natural preparation for a full kindergarten schedule — your child arrives already knowing how to be in school every single day.

The Honest Answer

There's no wrong choice. Every option represents the full Spark program. If you're unsure, start with two or three days and see how your child responds — it's not uncommon for families to add days mid-year once they see how their child is thriving.

Strategic Note

This section is the most conversion-critical on the page because it answers the #1 parent question before they have to ask it. Writing the scheduling choice as a developmental framing — rather than just a cost/logistics table — is child-centric and consistent with the 80/20 lens. The honest sign-off ("there's no wrong choice") is authentically Michelle's voice and builds trust.

Section 9 · Frequently Asked Questions
Questions About the 4's Program
What is the age requirement for the 4's program?
Children must be four years old by their first day of school and must be fully potty trained.
How do I choose the right number of days?
We recommend thinking about two things: your child's stamina and your family's schedule. Two days is a complete, rich program. Three days deepens peer relationships and curriculum engagement. Five days is ideal for children preparing for a full daily kindergarten schedule. If you're unsure, start with two or three — you can always add days.
Is the 4's program academically rigorous enough?
Yes. The Purposeful Play Framework is built on Dr. Peterson's doctoral research in early childhood education and the Experience Early Learning whole-child curriculum. The 4's program includes structured academic play, five enrichment disciplines, individualized learning paths, and developmental assessment. It is not a daycare program — it is a purposeful, research-grounded pre-K experience.
What's the class size?
Maximum 10–12 students per class with a 5:1 student-to-teacher ratio.
Can my child start mid-year?
Yes, subject to availability. Contact us to discuss your child's specific timing.
How will I know if my child is ready for kindergarten?
Dr. Peterson's individualized assessment approach means your child's development is tracked throughout the program — not just at year-end. You'll have ongoing communication with your child's teachers and a clear picture of their readiness as kindergarten approaches.
