What This Roadmap Covers

Every blog topic Spark Academy should publish over the next 18 months — organized into three phases and two parallel content tracks. Every topic is grounded in real keyword data, aligned with our dual-funnel conversion architecture, and written in your voice.

75 blog topics targeting over 120,000 monthly searches in aggregate. 57 parent-facing articles driving tour bookings. 18 educator-facing articles building your consulting pipeline. Every post is a new entry point into the funnel and a new reason for Google to consider Spark Academy the authority on early childhood education in Central Illinois.

Phase 1 (Months 1–6): 12 priority articles. Highest volume, lowest difficulty, greatest strategic impact. These launch the blog and establish topical authority.

Phase 2 (Months 7–12): 21 expansion articles. Deeper dives into parent concerns, enrichment topics, and the first wave of dedicated consulting-track posts.

Phase 3 (Months 13–18): 18 evergreen library articles. Long-tail topics, community content, and advanced educator thought leadership that compounds the authority built in Phases 1–2.

Seasonal Layer: 12 time-sensitive posts tied to enrollment windows, Camp Play registration, back-to-school, and year-end tax planning — republished annually.

Special Series: 12 posts across three branded series — "Inside the Classroom," "Ask Dr. Peterson," and "The Homeschool Hub" — designed for social sharing and email nurture campaigns.

Future Expansion: 24 additional topic ideas in the backlog, ready when capacity allows.

Decisions for Michelle

8 decisions to shape how this roadmap is implemented

Decision 1

Writing Workflow Preference

Determines how Stephen drafts each post and how much of your time each post requires.
Option A: Full Draft Review (15–20 min/post) — Stephen drafts, you review and add personal anecdotes.
Option B: Voice Memo + Draft (10–15 min/post) — You record a 5–10 min voice memo, Stephen builds the post.
Option C: Outline + Interview (25–30 min/post) — Stephen outlines, you fill in detail on a 15-min call.
You can mix and match by topic.
Decision 2

Downloadable PDF Checklists as Lead Magnets?

Articles 5 and 13 are natural lead magnets. Gating behind email capture builds a parent email list for nurture campaigns.
A: Yes — gate behind email capture
B: Yes — but ungated (no email required)
C: No — blog content only
Decision 3

Research Citations for Play-Based Learning Articles

Articles 8, 9, and 11 need specific research citations to maximize authority for Google.
A: You provide 2–3 study names/authors
B: Schedule a voice memo to discuss
C: Stephen drafts with general citations, you add specifics in review
Decision 4

Comfort Level with Educator-Facing Content

Articles 25–48 use professional vocabulary (NDBI, PBIS, improvement science). Please confirm these reflect your practice accurately.
A: Review topic list and flag any that don't align
B: Approve all as-is
C: Suggest additional topics
Decision 5

Homeschool Hub Series Priority

Discovery Station is a confirmed program. The Homeschool Hub series supports enrollment but is lower volume than core preschool content.

Publishing Cadence
