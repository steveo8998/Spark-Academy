Strategic Note
This is a seasonal conversion page with a built-in urgency mechanic: the May 30 pricing deadline. This deadline isn't manufactured scarcity; it's a real enrollment structure that rewards early commitment. Section 02

What Camp Play Is

Section Headline (H2)
A Perfect Blend of Structure and Freedom

Camp Play isn't daycare with a summer label. It's an intentionally designed program where children spend their mornings exploring, creating, experimenting, and playing — the same way they would during Spark's school year, but with the energy and spirit of summer woven through everything.

Every day, your child moves through a mix of hands-on activities designed for their age group: science experiments, art projects, cooking challenges, LEGO building, dramatic play, and outdoor games. The activities rotate. The groups stay small. And the adults in the room are the same credentialed teachers and staff who lead Spark's year-round programs — not seasonal hires filling seats.

Camp runs Monday through Friday, 8:30 AM to 12:00 PM. Your child arrives, dives into a morning packed with things they'll want to tell you about at lunch, and goes home ready for an afternoon nap or a backyard adventure.

Strategic Note
The "not daycare with a summer label" line is a structural cousin to the "not a daycare" positioning pillar that governs the preschool pages. It serves the same function here: immediate differentiation from chain summer programs and rec-department day camps that warehouse kids. The credentialed staff callout addresses the most common summer camp trust concern (who is actually watching my child?).

What Makes Camp Play Different

Section Headline (H2)
Not Every Summer Camp Is Built the Same

Small groups. Camp Play maintains the same small-group philosophy as Spark's school-year programs. Your child isn't one of fifty kids in a gymnasium. They're in a group matched to their age, with adults who know their name.

Credentialed staff. The people leading Camp Play are the same qualified, credentialed teachers and staff who run Spark Academy during the school year. This isn't a summer job for college students — it's an extension of the school your family already trusts (or is about to discover).

Play with purpose. Every activity is designed — not improvised. The science experiments, the art projects, the cooking challenges — they're chosen because they build something specific: curiosity, confidence, collaboration, and the kind of skills children carry into the school year ahead.

Led by Dr. Michelle Peterson, Ed.D. Camp Play operates under the same doctoral-level oversight as every other Spark program. Dr. Peterson designed the program structure, selected the activity framework, and leads the team. That continuity of quality is something most summer camps simply cannot offer.

Strategic Note
This section concentrates the school-centric proof layer. By this point on the page, the parent has been immersed in child-centric aspiration (hero, activities, age groups). Now they need reasons to trust. The four differentiators here are all verifiable — small groups, credentialed staff, intentional design, doctoral leadership — and each one separates Camp Play from chain summer programs and recreation-department offerings.

Section 08

Frequently Asked Questions

Section Headline (H2)
Questions About Camp Play
What ages can attend Camp Play?
Camp Play is open to children ages 3 through 11. Children are divided into four age-based groups (3/4, 5/6, 7/8, and 9–11) so every activity and interaction is matched to your child's developmental stage.
What are the hours?
Camp runs Monday through Friday, 8:30 AM to 12:00 PM. Drop-off begins at 8:30 and pickup is at noon.
Do I have to commit to the full summer?
No. You can register for individual days, individual weeks, or the full eight-week session. The pricing structure rewards longer commitments — but the day rate is always available for families who need flexibility.
What happens during the week of June 29 – July 3?
Camp is closed that week. Camp Play runs for eight total weeks between June 1 and July 31, with one week off for the Fourth of July holiday.
Is there a sibling discount?
Yes. Families with more than one child enrolled receive a 10% discount on the second child and beyond.
What if I register after May 30?
After May 30, weekly and full-summer pricing is no longer available. All registrations after that date (or once spots fill, whichever comes first) are at the day rate of $50 per day. Your child still gets the same Camp Play experience — the only difference is the rate.
