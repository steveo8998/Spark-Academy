Overview

What Changed in v5.0

In March 2026, Dr. Peterson reviewed three softer palette directions and selected Morning Mist — a lighter, cooler, more serene interpretation of the original Spark palette. The direction: keep the same color families (greens, golds, neutrals) but shift everything lighter and less saturated to create a "light and airy" website feel.

What Changed

System Neutrals (Section 2.1) and Primary Brand Colors (Section 2.2) — all web-facing structural colors updated to Morning Mist values.

What Did NOT Change

Logo System (Section 1), Secondary Brand Colors (Section 2.3 — logo-derived), Typography (Section 3), Photography Direction (Section 4). These remain identical to v3.0/v4.0.

Why: Dr. Peterson wanted the website to feel premium-serene rather than bold-saturated. Morning Mist achieves maximum visual lightness while maintaining WCAG AA accessibility compliance on all critical text-to-background combinations.

Section 4

Photography Direction

Avoid stock photography wherever possible. Real children in real classrooms are the most powerful conversion tool available. Every authentic Spark Academy photograph outperforms any stock image regardless of production quality. Unchanged in v5.0.

Style Standards

Natural Light

Shoot near windows during morning school hours when natural light is warmest and most diffused. Avoid overhead fluorescent lighting. Morning golden-hour light tonally aligns with the brand palette and communicates warmth and safety.

Warm Tone Processing

Maintain and enhance warm golden tones in post-processing. The brand palette is serene and cool-toned — photography provides the essential warmth that balances the Morning Mist color system. Avoid cool, blue-tinted, or desaturated processing.

Candid Over Posed

Capture children genuinely engaged — building, creating, reading with a teacher, exploring the developmental play room, playing outdoors. The most powerful photographs show complete absorption in activity. Never staged photo-shoot poses.

Teacher-Child Connection

Photographs of Dr. Peterson and Spark teachers in genuine one-on-one interaction with children are authority-building assets. A teacher kneeling to a child's level, helping with a project, or laughing together communicates everything the copy tries to say about relational education.

Environment Details

Classroom details matter to parents making enrollment decisions — materials on shelves, the developmental play room setup, outdoor spaces, the physical environment. Parents who can picture the space become far more likely to schedule a tour.

Do Not Use

✗ Stock photos of generic children — parents recognize stock photography and it immediately erodes trust
✗ Posed or staged setups — visible artificiality signals inauthenticity
✗ Cool, blue-tinted, or desaturated processing — conflicts with the warmth photography needs to provide in the Morning Mist system
✗ Sterile, cluttered, or institutional-looking environments
✗ Any image that could be mistaken for a daycare, chain, or corporate childcare center
Photography priority for Phase 5 build: Stephen should request 15–20 high-quality photographs from Dr. Peterson before development begins — ideally covering the classroom environment, teacher-child interaction, outdoor play, the developmental play room, and at least one image of Dr. Peterson with students. With the Morning Mist palette, warm photography is even more critical — the cooler color system relies on photographs to provide emotional warmth.
